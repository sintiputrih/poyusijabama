How Did Jeff Chandler Die?, Jeff Chandler Death and Cause

Jeff Chandler While working on Merrill’s Marauders in the Philippines, on April 15, 1961, he injured his back while playing baseball with U.S. Army Special Forces soldiers who worked as extras in the film. He had injections to deaden the pain and enable him to finish the production.

He entered a hospital in Culver City, California on May 13, 1961, and had surgery for a spinal disc herniation. There were severe complications; an artery was damaged, and Chandler hemorrhaged. In a seven-and-a-half-hour emergency operation over-and-above the original surgery, he was given 55 pints of blood on May 17. A third operation followed, on May 27, where he received an additional 20 pints of blood. Jeff Chandler died on June 17, 1961. The cause was a blood infection complicated by pneumonia.

At the time of his death, Chandler was involved with British actress Barbara Shelley. Tony Curtis and Gerald Mohr were among the pallbearers at Chandler’s funeral, attended by more than 1,500 people. He was buried at Hillside Memorial Park Cemetery in Culver City. An investigation ensued into Chandler’s death. It was deemed malpractice and resulted in his children suing the hospital for $1.5 million.

Chandler’s own estate was worth $600,000, which he left to his daughters. His ex-wife sued his estate for $80,000 for money owed under their divorce settlement. For his contribution to the motion picture industry, Chandler has a star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame, at 1770 Vine Street.

Who Is Jeff Chandler?, Jeff Chandler Biography and Wiki

Jeff Chandler was an American actor, singer, and film producer. Best known for his portrayal of Cochise in the 1950 western film, Broken Arrow. Notably, for which he was nominated for an Oscar. Undoubtedly, he was one of Universal Pictures’ most famous male stars of the 1950s.  Jeff also appeared in the films Sword in the Desert, Deported, Female on the Beach and A Story of David. He was born Ira Grossel in a Jewish family, Chandler was raised by his mother after his parents separated.

Jeff acted in plays while studying at Erasmus Hall High School. Following a drama course at Feagin School of Dramatic Art, he briefly worked in radio and then went on to serve in a stock company as a stage manager and actor.

Additional to his acting, Chandler was also known for his musical recordings. He had a number of affairs, on a personal note, before, during, and after his marriage to Marjorie Hoshelle, with whom he fathered two children.

Jeff Chandler Education and Childhood

Chandler was born as Ira Grossel on December 15, 1918, in Brooklyn, New York, USA, to Phillip Grossel and Anna. His parents separated when he was a child. He studied at Erasmus Hall High School where one of his schoolmates was Susan Hayward.

Jeff worked as a restaurant cashier and studied art for a year. He then took up a job as a layout artist at $18 a week to save enough money to take a drama course. Chandler eventually attended the Feagin School of Dramatic Art. He initially worked on the radio and then got employment in a stock agency as a stage manager and actor.

Jeff Chandler Shady Lane Playhouse

Chandler launched his own company called the Shady Lane Playhouse in 1941. Noteworthy, the company enjoyed some success with plays including The Bad Man and Seventh Heaven. Chandler enlisted in the army during World War II. He served for four years, finishing with the rank of lieutenant.

Jeff Chandler Actor and Career

Chandler got his first job as a radio actor in May 1946. Jeff received much fame for appearing as the lead in the radio series, Michael Shayne. In addition, he then became the first actor to play Chad Remington in the radio adventure drama, Frontier Town. Interestingly, his first film role was a one-line role as a criminal in Johnny O’Clock. It was released in 1947.

Jeff then went on to play a minor role as a policeman in the comedy film Mr. Belvedere Goes to College. He also played gangsters in The Invisible Wall and Roses are Red.

Jeff garnered more attention with the role of Eve Arden’s boyfriend in the radio show, Our Miss Brooks. It debuted on radio in 1948 and became a massive hit.

Jeff Chandler Universal Studio Career

Chandler was cast as an Israeli leader in Universal studio’s Sword in the Desert. This was the first American movie based on the first phase of the Civil War in Mandatory Palestine. Jeff featured in the noir adventure Smuggler’s Island (1948) as an adventurer. A role he liked as it was close to his personality.

He played a Polynesian leader in Bird of Paradise and a boxer in Iron Man in 1951. Jeff then made a war movie titled Red Ball Express. Chandler then signed a new contract with Universal in 1952. He then appeared in a western titled The Great Sioux Uprising. Then, he went on to work in the adventure flicks War Arrow and East of Sumatra. Thereafter, Jeff made the crime drama film Female on the Beach and began releasing musical records.

Jeff Chandler Broken Arrow

Chandler played Cochise in Delmer Daves’ western Broken Arrow. It turned out to be a hit, earning the actor an Oscar nomination. Ultimately, establishing him as a star.
