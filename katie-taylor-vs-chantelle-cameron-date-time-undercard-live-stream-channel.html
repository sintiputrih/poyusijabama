Katie Taylor will bid to bounce back from her first career defeat when she returns to Dublin to rematch Chantelle Cameron this evening.

The undisputed lightweight champion saw her undefeated record snapped in surprising fashion when she lost to 'Il Capo' in May.

Taylor's Dublin homecoming had been seven years in the making but her fairytale ending went up in smoke as she lost on points.

Taylor will have a chance to avenge her only defeat when she returns to Ireland for a second shot at Cameron's undisputed light-welterweight belts.

The 'Bray Bomber' is still aiming to become a two-weight undisputed champion but Cameron's undefeated record now stands at 18 wins and she will prove a fearsome challenge to overcome once again.

And at Friday's weigh-in, Taylor came in at 139.6lbs and Cameron was 139.5lbs, which is slightly less than both weighed during their first encounter.

Taylor vs Cameron 2: Date and how to follow

This super-lightweight title fight is scheduled to take place on Saturday, November 25.

It is being held again at the 3Arena in Dublin.

The main card is due to begin at 7pm, with ringwalks for the main event expected at around 10:30pm.

The fight will be shown live on DAZN in the UK.

Taylor vs Cameron 2: What has been said?

Taylor is raring to go and has been blown away by the support from the Irish crowd.

Speaking after the weigh-in, she said: "I just cannot wait to step in and fight. All I have to do now is to perform. I am very hungry and very excited.

“I don’t think I have ever been this motivated for a fight to be honest. I can’t wait to step in there tomorrow night. I love my job. I love being involved in these big fights. I don’t mind been the underdog. I just can’t wait.

“I put my body through the trenches. Once I perform, I think it is going to be enough.

“It is unbelievable [the support]. It is so humbling to see them each and every time. It just grateful and hopefully I will give them something to celebrate tomorrow night."
