Karolína Muchová boyfriend-Czech professional tennis player, Karolína Muchová was born on August 21, 1996 in Olomouc in Czech Republic.

Josef Mucha, a former Czech football player, is her father. At the age of seven, he taught her how to play tennis. She also has a brother, and when they were younger, they played a lot of sports together.

When she was around 12 years old, she decided to switch from handball to tennis because there were tennis courts close to her house.

She relocated to Prague in 2019 to train. She cited Roger Federer as her childhood tennis hero. She battled numerous injuries throughout her junior year.

Karolína Muchová’s Career

At the age of 17, Muchová started competing on the ITF Women’s Circuit in October 2013. In Dubrovnik, she competed in a $10k competition, where she advanced to the second round.

She won her maiden ITF championship at Michalovce, Slovakia, in July of the following year. After that, she began to perform poorly until March 2016, when she won her second singles ITF championship in Sharm El Sheikh and two weeks later another competition there.

She competed in the $75,000 ITS Cup event in Olomouc in July 2017 and lost to her countrywoman Markéta Vondrouová in the final.

She lost to Karolna Plková in the opening round of the Australian Open to begin the 2019 campaign. After defeating Samantha Stosur and Hsieh Su-wei at the Premier-level Qatar Ladies Open, she advanced to the WTA Tour quarterfinals for the first time, but ultimately fell to No. 4 seed Elina Svitolina.

She made her Premier Mandatory level event debut in the Miami Open, however Angelique Kerber defeated her in the second round.

Muchová’s performance in 2020 was fairly inconsistent. For the first time in her career, she advanced to the second round of the Australian Open before falling to CiCi Bellis in straight sets.

She subsequently competed for the first time in the Qatar Ladies Open, where she also defeated Magda Linette for the first time but lost in round two to Kiki Bertens, the seventh seed.

Muchová defeated world No. 1 Ashleigh Barty to go to the Australian Open quarterfinals, where she was defeated by Jennifer Brady in three sets.

Muchová earned a career-high ranking of world No. 19 on 17 May 2021 after defeating the second seed Naomi Osaka and the 16th seed Maria Sakkari in the quarterfinals of the Madrid Open, her first appearance at the WTA 1000 quarterfinal level.

She continued her strong play two weeks later, in June, when she advanced to the third round of the French Open for the first time in her career.

Muchová was an unseeded player when she returned to Miami. She overcame fellow Czech Tereza Martincová in the opening round after two tie-break sets.

She then defeated the 18th seed Leylah Fernandez before pulling out of her match against Naomi Osaka in the third round.

Using her protected ranking, she returned to the 2023 Australian Open and advanced to the second round.

She also used her protected status to advance to the quarterfinals at Dubai, where she defeated eighth seed Belinda Bencic, but withdrew from the quarterfinal match. On February 27, 2023, after climbing 35 ranks, she returned to the top 100.

She became the fifth player from the Czech Republic to compete in the Open Era final at Roland Garros. She was defeated in three sets by world No. 1 and defending champion Iga Witek in the championship match. As a result, on June 12, 2023, she achieved a career-high rating of No. 16.
