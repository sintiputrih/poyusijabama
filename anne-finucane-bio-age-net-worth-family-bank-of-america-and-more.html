Anne Finucane Biography

Anne Finucane (b. 12 July 1952) is an American banker who currently serves as the vice-chair of Bank of America in charge of the bank’s environmental, social and corporate governance committee. She joined Bank of America in 1995 and also serves on boards of several organizations. She currently lives in Lincoln, MA with her husband Mike Barnicle.

Anne leads the Bank of America’s environmental, social and corporate governance committee. She joined Fleet Financial in 1995. Fleet later merged with the short-lived BankBoston to become part of Bank of America. Following the completion of her studies at the University of New Hampshire, Anne Finucane worked in the mayor’s arts office.

She has been on the boards of The American Ireland Fund, Brigham and Women’s Hospital, Carnegie Hall, CVS Health the International Center for Journalists, the John F. Kennedy Presidential Library, Boston Public Library Foundation and Museum of Fine Arts, Boston, the John F. Kennedy Library Foundation, Partners HealthCare, and the Special Olympics. Besides, Anne Finucane is a member of the Council on Foreign Relations and has been president of the Massachusetts Women’s Forum.

Anne Finucane Bank Of America

Anne Finucane entered the banking industry when she joined Fleet Financial in 1995, serving as head of corporate affairs and marketing. She worked to improve the bank’s reputation following a subprime lending crisis and helped in company acquisitions. Fleet Financial later merged with BankBoston, then Bank of America.

Anne Finucane became FleetBoston Financial’s executive vice president for corporate marketing and communications. She was Bank of America’s global chief strategy and marketing officer after the financial crisis of 2007–2008 when the bank lost 55% of its value, “symbolizing all that was wrong” with US banks.

Anne Finucane became the company’s vice chair in 2015. She also chairs the company’s environmental, social and corporate governance committee. Moreover, Anne leads customer analytics, global marketing, and public policy strategies at the Bank of America. She also chairs the Bank of American Charitable Foundation. According to American Banker, Anne Finucane works with the White House and the World Economic Forum.

In mid-2017, Anne Finucane was reportedly considered for Uber’s vacant chief executive officer position, following the resignation of Travis Kalanick. In September 2017, Anne led the Bank of America’s European bank board.

Honors

Anne Finucane ranked sixth on Boston‘s list of “The 100 Women Who Run This Town” in 2010. She has also ranked highly on lists of influential American women, including American Banker‘s “most powerful women” lists in 2015 (12th), 2016 (10th), and 2017 (9th). Anne is in Fortune‘s “most powerful women” list, on which she ranked 50th in 2016 and 2017. The American banker was included in PRWeek‘s 2013 “Power List”, and ranked number 20 in Forbes‘ 2014 list of “50 Most Influential CMOs” in the world.

Other honors include induction into the Academy of Distinguished Bostonians by the Greater Boston Chamber of Commerce, and a Matrix Award from the Association for Women in Communications‘ New York affiliate, both in 2013. Additionally, the Roman Catholic Archdiocese of Boston’s Planning Office for Urban Affairs honored Anne Finucane for her “commitment and work in the name of social justice” in 2017.
