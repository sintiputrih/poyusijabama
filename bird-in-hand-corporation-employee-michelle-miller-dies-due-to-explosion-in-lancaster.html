Bird-in-Hand Corporation Employee Michelle Miller dies due to explosion in Lancaster

 Posted on July 9, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  313 words  |  Jenniffer Sheldon

In a heart-wrenching turn of events, the community is grappling with the sudden passing of Michelle Miller, who was confirmed dead on Tuesday, December 19, 2023. The news has left a trail of sorrow, with many hearts scarred by the unexpected departure of a woman who touched the lives of all fortunate enough to know her.

Since the shocking revelation of her death, an outpouring of condolences has swept across the community. Tributes and heartfelt statements are pouring in, testifying to the impact Michelle had on the lives of those around her. The community unites in urging everyone to keep Michelle Miller’s family in their thoughts and prayers as they navigate through this sorrowful time, grappling with the unimaginable loss.

The circumstances surrounding Michelle’s sudden demise were unveiled on Tuesday when Bird-in-Hand Corporation disclosed that the employee in question was a 61-year-old woman named Michelle Miller from Lancaster. With an impressive 34-year tenure with the organization, she held the vital position of night auditor at the Inn.

On that fateful Tuesday, people from all corners of the region flocked to the inn, witnessing the aftermath of the devastation that occurred on Old Philadelphia Pike. As the inn’s proprietors grapple with the tragedy and contemplate their next steps, the hotel, previously closed for repairs and cleaning, will remain shuttered until further notice.

Janice Ballenger, a colleague and friend, expressed, “She would be the FIRST person to offer a prayer up for someone. Please do the same for her family & friends. Michelle was our night auditor, friend, and one of the chambers of the BIH.”

In memory of Michelle Miller, a candlelight vigil will be held on Thursday at 6 pm on the Bird-in-Hand Family Inn front lawn. The community is invited to gather at the Farmers Market for parking, joining together to pay respects and remember the life of a remarkable woman.
